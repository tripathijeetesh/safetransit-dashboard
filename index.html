GWR METRIC DEFINITIONS
Mean Local R² (0.529): Average proportion of collision rate variance explained by the GWR model across all block groups. 52.9% of spatial variation in collision risk is captured — a 51% improvement over the non-spatial OLS baseline.
AICc (3,580): Corrected Akaike Information Criterion — measures model fit quality. Lower is better. Used to compare GWR against OLS and confirm that spatial variation in coefficients is statistically justified.
OLS Baseline R² (0.351): The non-spatial global regression R². GWR (0.529) outperforms this by 51%, proving that behavior-collision relationships vary significantly by location.
Block Groups (1,609): US Census block groups used as the spatial unit of analysis across the DC-VA-MD study area. Each block group has its own locally-estimated GWR coefficient.

Model Glossary — Terms & Definitions
4 MODELS IN USE
ARIMAX(3,0,3) — Time Series Forecasting
AutoRegressive Integrated Moving Average with eXogenous inputs. The notation (3,0,3) means: 3 autoregressive lags (past 3 months of collisions predict the current month), 0 differencing (data is already stationary), and 3 moving-average lags (accounts for 3 months of past forecast errors). Bus video events are the exogenous input — they act as an early warning signal (β=0.083, p<0.01). Used for: Monthly collision rate forecasting and 30-day ahead predictions.
Random Forest — Ensemble Risk Classifier
An ensemble of 100 decision trees trained on bootstrapped data samples, each using a random subset of features. Predictions are made by majority vote across all trees, which reduces overfitting compared to a single tree. Accuracy: 78.3% (F1=0.78). Used for: Classifying each transit zone into risk tiers (High / Med-High / Moderate / Low) based on behavior and environmental features. Outperforms the 75% accuracy target.
XGBoost — Best-Performing Classifier ✓
Extreme Gradient Boosting builds 300 trees sequentially — each new tree corrects the residual errors of the previous one (learning rate=0.1). This iterative correction yields higher accuracy than Random Forest's parallel approach. Accuracy: 81.2% (F1=0.81). Used for: Final zone risk classification. Deployed as the primary decision model. Also provides feature importance rankings to identify which behaviors drive risk most strongly.
GWR — Spatial Regression (Spatial Risk Tab)
Geographically Weighted Regression estimates a separate regression coefficient for each of the 1,609 census block groups, using an adaptive exponential kernel with 65 neighbors. Unlike global OLS (R²=0.351), GWR captures spatial variation in behavior-collision relationships (Mean R²=0.529, +51%). Used for: Identifying WHERE each driver behavior has the strongest association with collisions — powers the behavior heatmaps on the Spatial Risk tab.
Full Time Series Chart — What It Shows
Monthly view of bus video event volume (right axis, dashed amber) plotted alongside collision rate per VRM (left axis, solid blue) from Jan 2020 – Dec 2023. The visual co-movement between rising video events and subsequent collision spikes motivates using video events as the exogenous input in ARIMAX. The sharp 2022 surge in video events corresponds with expanded in-vehicle camera deployment.
ARIMAX Model Parameters — What They Mean
The parameter table shows AR/MA lag coefficients (autoregressive and moving-average terms) and the exogenous coefficient β=0.083 (p<0.01) for bus video events. A positive β confirms that higher video event volume statistically predicts higher collision rates 1–3 days ahead — making it a leading indicator suitable for proactive supervisor dispatch. AIC=4,027 confirms a good model fit after adding the exogenous term.
How the 4 models work together: ARIMAX handles temporal forecasting (when will collisions spike?). GWR identifies spatial behavior-collision relationships (where and which behaviors matter most?). Random Forest and XGBoost classify current zone risk tiers (what is the risk level right now?). Together they form a complete early-warning system — from leading indicators to geographic targeting to actionable risk scores.
